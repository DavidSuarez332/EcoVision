EcoVision

Automated Solar Panel Cleanliness Detection Using Deep Learning and AlexNet-Based Computer Vision

Problem & Motivation

Solar photovoltaic (PV) arrays experience significant efficiency loss due to surface contamination such as dust, pollen, ash, mineral deposits, and bird droppings. Studies show that accumulated debris can reduce energy output by 15–25% annually.

Manual inspection and cleaning cycles are labor-intensive and cost-inefficient. EcoVision provides an automated solution using deep learning to classify panel cleanliness from imagery.

Technical Approach (AlexNet-Based Classification)

  • Input normalization and resizing to 224×224 pixels.
  • Data augmentation: random flips, rotations, brightness shifts, contrast jitter.
  • Training for 10–20 epochs using SGD with momentum.
  • Monitoring training/validation loss curves.
  • Evaluating robustness via confusion matrix analysis.

Results

EcoVision achieved 85–95% classification accuracy depending on augmentation and dataset composition.

Key Takeaways

  • Deep learning is scalable for solar panel inspection.
  • AlexNet effectively captures dirt-related textures.
  • EcoVision reduces operational costs and improves PV performance.
  • Approach can extend to other renewable energy monitoring tasks.
